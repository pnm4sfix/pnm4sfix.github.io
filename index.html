About

I am a neuroscientist specializing in cognitive neuroscience and computational modeling. My research focuses on understanding the neural mechanisms underlying learning, memory, and decision-making processes. I am currently seeking postdoctoral fellowship opportunities to further investigate how neural circuits adapt to complex cognitive tasks.

I completed my Ph.D. at [University Name], where I developed novel experimental paradigms combining behavioral studies with neuroimaging techniques. My work integrates computational modeling, electrophysiology, and advanced statistical methods to uncover the principles governing neural computation.

Research Statement

My research program aims to elucidate the neural computations that enable flexible, adaptive behavior. I employ a multi-faceted approach combining:

  • Behavioral experiments to characterize cognitive processes
  • Neuroimaging (fMRI, EEG) to map neural activity patterns
  • Computational modeling to formalize mechanistic hypotheses
  • Statistical inference to test predictions and validate models

My postdoctoral research will build on these foundations to investigate how neural systems integrate information across multiple timescales and modalities. I am particularly interested in developing new analytical frameworks that bridge cellular, circuit, and systems-level neuroscience.
