About

I am an early career neuroscientist and my research interests are in neural coding, computation and excitability. Throughout my research career, I have acquired a wide range of techniques to visualise and record the activity of neurons in the brain and nervous system. I have a passion for neural data analysis, combining cutting edge microscopy and electrophysiology and novel analytical methods to improve our understanding the brain. My aim is to use and develop this skillset to address fundamental questions in neuroscience.

I completed my Ph.D. at University of Leeds and currently lead a Leverhulme Early Career Fellowship at the University of St Andrews. My work integrates computational modeling, electrophysiology, microscopy and advanced data analysis methods to uncover the principles governing neural computation.
